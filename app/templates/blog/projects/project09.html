ScreenBloom

screenbloom.com
ScreenBloom Repository
Technologies Used:

Python, Flask, HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery

ScreenBloom is an application that parses an image of your current PC screen and extracts the average color of each pixel. That color is then sent to your connected Philips Hue bulbs to create a subtle Ambilight-like effect.

I've always loved my gimmicky Philips Hues, and wanted to develop a program for them even before I got into programming. Fast-forward to February 2015: the time was right for a new larger-scale project, and to my delight I found the Philips Hue API to be extremely well designed and a pleasure to work with.

I had always been curious to replicate Philips' Ambilight effect available on many of their TVs, but was not able to find a suitable third-party solution, so I set about developing my own.

The program is fully functional and works extremely well (much to my initial surprise). It's great to throw on for a movie, where vibrant scenes will extend out into your living room. I think the program really shines when used with video games, though, as the lighting will gradually shift with the perspective of your character. The effect is subtle but really fosters immersion, especially with settings like dynamic brightness enabled that will dim the lights as your character enters a cave.

If you have some Hues, you can head over to screenbloom.com and download the application for yourself!
